# Privacy Policy — Mark Clipper

**Last updated: 2026-06-30**

Mark Clipper does not collect, transmit, or sell any of your data. There are no
accounts, no servers, no analytics, and no API keys.

## What the extension reads

To capture page content, Mark Clipper reads the page you are currently viewing —
its text, tables, and metadata — at the moment you click a capture button. It can
read any page you visit because it auto-follows your active tab; this is what lets
the side panel always capture the page in front of you. Reading a page is not the
same as sending it anywhere. Nothing read from a page is uploaded.

## What the extension stores

Your editor content is saved on your own computer using the browser's local
storage (`storage.local`). This stays on your machine, is never synced to any
account or cloud, and survives a browser restart. You can clear it at any time
with the Clear button, or by removing the extension.

## What leaves your machine

Only what you choose to send. When you use "Copy & Open," the extension copies
text to your clipboard and opens an AI chat in a new tab — *you* paste it
yourself. The extension never auto-submits anything and never makes network
requests of its own.

## Contact

Mark Clipper is open source. Questions or concerns can be raised on the project's
repository.
